¿Procesamiento predictivo?

"It is the theory that the brain is a sophisticated hypothesis-testing mechanism, which is constantly involved in minimizing the error of its predictions of the sensory input it receives from the world. This mechanism is meant to explain percep- tion and action and everything mental in between. It is an attractive theory because powerful theoretical arguments support it. It is also attractive because more and more empirical evidence is beginning to point in its favour. It has enormous unifying power and yet it can explain in detail too." (Howhy 2013, 1)
PP: La hipótesis de que el cerebro es un mechamismo que minimiza el predictivo error.

En el caso de la percepción, tenemos que el error predictivo = estímulo sensorial recibido menos estímulo sensorial predecido

Pero además, según Hohwy el PP nos da más
“the idea that the brain minimizes its prediction error [...] explains not just that we perceive but how we perceive: the idea applies directly to key aspects of the phenomenology of perception.” (2013, 1).
Qualification: it gives us constraints: we have not yet a complete PP model -it is more of a general framework; and neither do we have an uncontroversial phenomenological description of perception. But on the basis of PP, we can place constraints on the possible ways in which we can talk about a relation between brain processing and phenomenology.

El horizonte temporal

En esta sección explicaré a qué me refiero con la fenomenología de la percepción temporal
Experiencing successions of events, as with a series of notes comprising a melody, poses a puzzle. It seems that neither experiencing the different notes simultaneously nor experiencing them in sequence can give rise to the experience of succession. If we experience all notes simultaneously, we experience not a melody but a chord. If we experience first one note, then another, this is a succession of experiences, not an experience of succession (cf. James 1890). So how can we conceive of the experience of successions of events, and of temporally extended processes in general? (Wiese 2017)
An important example derives from our experience of listening to music. When we listen to music, including when we hear a piece played for the first time, we don’t hear each note in isolation, one after the other. Rather, when we hear a note of the melody, the phenomenal character of our experience of that note is affected by the properties of the notes immediately prior to that note. This is not that surprising, and may be an effect of a kind of remembered experience affecting the character of one’s current experience. But what is surprising is that when we hear a note of the melody, the phenomenal character of our experience of that note is affected by the properties of the notes immediately after that note. So our experience of the note is affected by what is yet to come as well as what has already been. (Paul 2014)
What you experience, rather, is, to a first approximation, the rising of the current sounds out of the past; you hear the current sounds as surging forth from the past. You hear them as a continuation. This is to say, moving on to a better approximation, you hear them as having a certain trajectory or arc, as unfolding in accordance with a definite law or pattern. It is not the past that is present in the current experience; rather, it is the trajectory or arc that is present now, and of course the arc describes the relation of what is now to what has already happened (and to what may still happen). In this way, what is present, strictly speaking, refers to or is directed toward what has happened and what will happen. (Noë 2006, p. 29, quote by Wiese 2017, 7)
Este tipo de fenómenos se puede apreciar en el caso de la siguiente ilusión.

¿Qué se siente cuando 2 ya ha pasado y 3 todavía no?

Dar cuenta de la fenomenología tempora es, al menos, dar cuenta de estos fenómenos.

Wiese: el horizonte temporal y el procesamiento predictivo

En una impresionante colección de artículos sobre filosofía y procesamiento predictivo, Wanja Wiese presenta una respuesta a la pregunta por cómo dar cuenta de la experiencia temporal desde un modelo PP.
Para eso toma el modelo de la representación temporal de Rick Grush (Trajectory Estimate Model) y lo adopta al PP para presentar HiTEM (Hierarchy of Trajectory Estimates Model).

Voy a explicar rápidamente los modelos

La idea es que una experiencia temporal en un tiempo t está definida por una trayectoria de las experiencias entre entre los tiempos [t - j] y [t + k].
El problema, dice Wiese, es que esto no logra dar cuenta de los casos que hemos presentado como casos de un mismo fenómeno.

Grush entiende el caso del conejo cutáneo como un caso perceptual y el caso de la música como un caso de memoria

‘At the time of the second impulse, the subject perceives it to be at the wrist; at the time of the (sixth) impulse, the subject has no recollection of this prior interpretation and rather has a perceptual state to the effect that there is currently a sequence of impulses; the second of which was just proximal to the wrist’.
One, which potentially spans a larger interval, might be described as conceptual in the sense that it is a matter of interpreting present experience in terms of concepts of processes that span potentially large intervals. Music appreciation would fall into this category. When I recognize something as part of a larger whole (a spatial whole or a temporal whole), then my concept of that whole influences the content grasped via the part. Something along these lines is what appears to be happening with music. (Grush 2006)
Sugerencia de Wiese:

Misma jerarquía pero ahora sin división. Es una jerarquía, típica del PP. La idea de Wiese tomar el modelo de PP tiene una jerarquía de representaciones y ajustarlo al modelo de Grush.

La idea de Wiese es que una experiencia temporal en un tiempo t está definida por la jerarquía de trayectorias de las experiencias entre entre los tiempos [t - j] y [t + k].
The content of [temporal experience] is not best conceived as a linear stream of events, but rather as a hierarchy of temporal wholes. (Wiese 2017, 1)

Un problema

¿Cuál es el contenido de esas representaciones?

Esta es una pregunta abierta a la que en el momento se le da varios tipos de respuesta.

Problema. Si seleccionamos UNA (e.g., la hipótesis que tenga mayor probabilidad en la jerarquía.) de la jeraquía entonces perdemos la relación entre los elementos de la jerarquía. Pero justo habíamos dicho que para dar cuenta de la fenomenología del tiempo teníamos que dar cuenta de las relaciones entre representaciones del pasado, presente y futuro.

Si seleccionamos varias: no queda claro cómo se determinaría la fenomenología ya que no tenemos un contenido particular. Quedamos en el problema de que parece que la fenomenología no parece mostrarnos una jerarquía.

Intento de solución:

¡sin representaciones!

Tenemos que dar cuenta del modelo en términos necesaramiente relacionales –en particular, de las formas en que el sujeto se adapta a un ambiente a través del tiempo.
